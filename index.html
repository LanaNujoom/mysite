Birds Types

Birds (Aves) are a group of vertebrates which evolved from dinosaurs. They are endothermic, with feathers.

Birds live all over the world. They range in size from the 5 cm (2 in) bee hummingbird to the 2.70 m (9 ft) ostrich. They are the class of tetrapods with the most living species: about ten thousand. More than half of these are passerines, sometimes known as perching birds.

Birds Types

- Waterfowl Birds

Waterfowl, in the United States, all varieties of ducks, geese, and swans; the term is sometimes expanded to include some unrelated aquatic birds such as coots, grebes (see photograph), and loons. In Britain the term refers only to domesticated swans, geese, and ducks kept for ornamental purposes, wildfowl being the term used for wild birds of this group, especially in the context of shooting for sport. See also duck; goose; swan.

- Seriemas Birds

Seriema, also called cariama, South American bird of grasslands, constituting the family Cariamidae (order Gruiformes). There are two species, both restricted to southern-central South America. The red-legged, or crested, seriema (Cariama cristata), with long legs and neck, stands about 60 cm (2 feet) tall.

- Diurnal Birds of Prey (Accipitriformes)

The diurnal birds of prey are formally classified into five families of two orders. Accipitridae: hawks, eagles, buzzards, harriers, kites, and Old World vultures. Pandionidae: the osprey.
